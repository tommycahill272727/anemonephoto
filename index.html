Day 12 — Somewhere above the treeline, Iceland

Fallen
Light

Seventeen days along the Arctic coastline. The light here doesn't rise or fall the way light should — it drags, slides, lingers on the horizon like it can't quite commit to leaving. The kind of light that makes you stop walking and just stand there, cold, not caring.

I shot three rolls before breakfast. None of them will be any good. That's okay. Some things you shoot just to prove you were there.

note to self — bring more film next time. obviously.
